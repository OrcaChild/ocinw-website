## Keeping Our Coastline Clean, One Beach at a Time

The SoCal Beach Cleanup Program is Orca Child in the Wild's flagship conservation project. Every first Saturday of the month, we gather volunteers of all ages along the Southern California coast -- from the sandy stretches of Santa Monica down to the shores of Imperial Beach -- to remove trash, plastic debris, and other pollutants before they can harm marine life. Since launching in April 2026, this program has become one of the most welcoming entry points for families, students, and community members who want to make a direct, visible difference for our ocean.

## What Happens at a Cleanup

Each cleanup event kicks off with a short orientation where experienced team leads explain safety guidelines, how to sort collected items, and what kinds of debris to watch for. Volunteers spread out across designated beach zones armed with reusable gloves, buckets, and data cards. Every piece of trash gets logged -- cigarette butts, bottle caps, food wrappers, fishing line -- so we can track pollution trends over time and advocate for smarter waste policies. After the sweep, we gather for a group photo, share fun facts about the marine species that live in the area, and celebrate the difference we made together. The whole event runs about two hours, and yes, there are snacks.

## Why This Work Matters

Plastic pollution is one of the biggest threats facing ocean wildlife along the Southern California coast. Sea turtles mistake plastic bags for jellyfish. Shorebirds feed bottle caps to their chicks. Microplastics work their way into the food chain, affecting everything from plankton to dolphins. By removing trash before it breaks down, our volunteers are directly protecting the habitats of gray whales, California sea lions, garibaldi fish, and dozens of shorebird species that depend on clean coastlines to feed, rest, and raise their young. The data we collect also feeds into regional research efforts led by our partners at Surfrider Foundation, Heal the Bay, and I Love A Clean San Diego, helping scientists and lawmakers understand where pollution is worst and what solutions will have the greatest impact.

## How to Get Involved

Getting involved is easy and free. No experience is needed -- just bring sunscreen, a reusable water bottle, and a willingness to get your hands a little sandy. We welcome individuals, families, school groups, and scout troops. Volunteers under 13 are welcome with a parent or guardian present. Check our events page for the next scheduled cleanup location, sign up through our volunteer form, and we will send you all the details. If you cannot make it to a beach day, you can still help by organizing a cleanup at your local park, creek, or lake using our downloadable cleanup toolkit. Every piece of trash you pick up is one less piece in the ocean.
